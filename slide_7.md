# Slide 7 Specification

## Slide Purpose
Introduce the **first actionable behavior change** after the mindset shift in Slide 6.

Slide 6 reframed the problem as:
- not prompting vs directing

Slide 7 answers:
**What should I actually do differently right now?**

This slide should feel:
- simple
- actionable
- immediately usable

It should NOT:
- introduce full systems or templates
- overwhelm with detail
- become technical

## Core Idea (locked)

Don’t build yet  
Ask how it should work first

## On-Slide Text (locked)

Don’t rush to build  
Ask how it should work first

Keep to exactly two lines.

## Visual Design (locked)

### Primary Visual: Flow Shift

A simple left-to-right flow that contrasts two approaches.

#### Top / Old Flow (faint or smaller):
Describe → Build

#### Bottom / New Flow (primary emphasis):
Describe → Plan → Build

The "Plan" step should be visually emphasized:
- slightly larger
- highlighted
- centered

### Layout
- Full-screen slide
- Text centered at top or middle
- Flow diagram centered beneath text
- Strong whitespace

### Visual Principles
- minimal
- clean
- no clutter
- no memes
- simple shapes (boxes/arrows)

## Typography
- very large font (>= 40pt equivalent)
- bold or semi-bold
- clean sans serif

## Color
- neutral background
- high contrast text
- "Plan" step can be subtly highlighted (accent color)
- avoid red (this is not a failure moment)

## Animation

### Entrance
- "Describe → Build" appears first
- then "Plan" inserts itself between them
- final state becomes: Describe → Plan → Build

### Behavior
- smooth insertion animation for "Plan"
- keep transitions subtle and fast

## Speaker Script (guideline)

Say approximately:

"Here’s the small change that makes a huge difference…"

"Most people describe what they want…"

"And then immediately ask AI to build it."

(point to Describe → Build)

"That only works for simple things."

(pause)

"For launchable products, you must.. "

"Ask the AI how it should work."

(point to Describe → Plan → Build)

Then expand slightly:

"What’s the best way to structure this?"

"How should the pieces connect?"

"How do we keep this consistent as it grows?"

## Timing and Delivery
- quick pacing at start
- pause after "Don’t do that"
- slight emphasis on "Plan"
- do not go deep into examples yet

## Transition from Slide 6

Slide 6 ends with:
"You have to start directing it."

Then say:

"Here’s the first way you do that…"

Slide 7 appears.

## Transition to Slide 8

After explaining:

"And once you do that…"

"Now you have something to keep consistent."

This leads into the consistency / reuse slide.

## Do Not
- do not show long prompts
- do not include AGENTS.md or docs examples here
- do not add technical terms (DRY, architecture, etc.)
- do not overload with text

## Success Criteria
The slide is successful if:
- audience understands the new step (Plan)
- audience feels this is easy to try
- the flow change is visually obvious
- it sets up deeper examples later without overwhelming them
