# Slide 3 Specification

## Slide Purpose
This slide converts the Dory analogy into a clear consequence.

Slide 2 established: "Eagerly helpful / Can’t follow the plot."
Slide 3 answers: **So what happens because of that?**

The job here is to make the outcome obvious and memorable without introducing jargon.

## Core Idea (locked)

Starts strong  
Never reaches the ending

## On-Slide Text (locked)

Starts strong  
Never reaches the ending

Keep it to exactly two lines. No additional words.

## Visual Design (locked)

### Primary Visual: Story Arc Line
- A simple horizontal story arc/curve
- Left side (start) is clear, solid, and confident
- The arc rises through the middle
- Before reaching the end, the line **fades, fragments, or breaks**
- The end point is implied but **not reached**

This visual should immediately communicate: "the story never finishes."

### Layout
- Full-screen slide
- Minimal elements
- Text centered or slightly above center
- Story arc runs horizontally across the slide, behind or just below the text

### Visual Principles
- very high whitespace
- no clutter
- no memes on this slide
- no extra icons competing with the core message

## Typography
- very large font (>= 40pt equivalent)
- bold or semi-bold
- clean sans serif
- each line treated as a headline

## Color
- neutral background
- high contrast text
- subtle visual degradation toward the right side of the arc
- do NOT overuse red; keep emphasis restrained and focused on the break

## Animation

### Entrance
- story arc appears first (subtle fade)
- text appears immediately after

### Behavior
- calm, controlled
- no flashy transitions
- this slide should feel like clarity after analogy

## Speaker Script (guideline)

Say approximately:

"So every moment makes sense…  
but the story goes nowhere."

(pause, let it land)

"That’s what’s happening when you build this way."

"You don’t fail at the beginning…  
you fail as you try to finish."

Then translate to product language:

"In product terms—  
you’re great at getting to a prototype…  
but you struggle to get to something you can actually build into an MVP."

## Timing and Delivery
- brief pause after first line
- allow recognition moment
- slight emphasis on "finish"

Do not rush this transition.

## Transition from Slide 2

Slide 2 ends with:
"…but the story goes nowhere."

Slide 3 should appear immediately after this line.

The audience should feel:
- recognition → consequence

## Transition to Slide 4 (Credibility)

After delivering the product translation, move into credibility:

"I kept running into this across everything I built with AI…"

This sets up the next slide (your experience) naturally.

## Do Not
- do not introduce technical terms on the slide
- do not add bullets
- do not clutter the visual
- do not add memes
- do not explain causes yet

## Success Criteria
The slide is successful if:
- the audience understands the consequence immediately
- the phrase "never reaches the ending" sticks
- the audience connects story → product without confusion
- it cleanly sets up your credibility and the deeper explanation to come
