# Slide 8 Specification

## Slide Purpose
Explain why systems break **over time** even after good initial planning, and introduce the need to continuously reinforce what matters.

Slide 7 taught:
- Insert a planning step before building

Slide 8 teaches:
- Planning once is not enough
- As the system grows, focus degrades

This slide answers:
**Why does everything still fall apart even when you plan well?**

## Core Idea (locked)

Stay focused  
Reinforce what matters as things grow

## On-Slide Text (locked)

Stay focused  
Reinforce what matters as things grow

Keep to exactly two lines.

## Visual Design (locked)

### Primary Visual: Growth vs Drift

A side-by-side visual showing two systems as they expand.

#### Left Side (Drift)
- A system that starts simple
- Expands outward into more nodes / elements
- Connections become loose or inconsistent
- Some branches misaligned or missing links
- Overall structure becomes messy or unclear

#### Right Side (Aligned Growth)
- Same starting system
- Expands similarly
- But core structure remains intact
- Clear anchor points or constraints keep things aligned
- Connections remain consistent and intentional

### Visual Message
Growth alone creates chaos.  
Growth + reinforcement maintains coherence.

### Layout
- Full-screen slide
- Text centered near top
- Visual comparison below (left vs right)
- Strong whitespace

### Visual Principles
- clean and minimal
- no clutter
- no memes
- same base shapes on both sides so difference is structure, not content

## Typography
- large font (>= 40pt equivalent)
- bold or semi-bold
- clean sans serif

## Color
- neutral base
- left side slightly more chaotic / lower contrast
- right side more structured / higher contrast
- subtle accent to show "anchors" or constraints
- avoid heavy red (not a failure moment, more of a realization)

## Animation

### Entrance
- base system appears (center)
- branches expand outward on both sides

### Behavior
- left side gradually becomes messy / inconsistent
- right side stays organized as it grows
- subtle animation to show divergence over time

No flashy transitions.

## Speaker Script (guideline)

Say approximately:

"Even if you plan the system…"

"There’s another problem."

(pause)

"As the context grows… the AI loses focus."

(point to left side)

"It stops prioritizing the important ideas…"

"And starts making decisions that are locally correct…"

"…but globally inconsistent."

(point to right side)

"So your job isn’t just to plan once…"

"It’s to keep reinforcing what matters."

Then tie to real examples:

"That’s why reactions showed up in one place but not another…"

"That’s why inputs drifted…"

"That’s why logic broke over time…"

## Timing and Delivery
- pause after "loses focus"
- emphasize "locally correct, globally inconsistent"
- keep pacing steady, not rushed

## Transition from Slide 7

Slide 7 ends with:
"Ask how it should work first"

Then say:

"And even if you do that…"

Slide 8 appears.

## Transition to Slide 9

After explaining:

"So what does this actually look like in practice?"

This sets up the prompting evolution slide.

## Do Not
- do not introduce technical jargon
- do not add extra text
- do not show code
- do not clutter the visual

## Success Criteria
The slide is successful if:
- audience understands why systems degrade over time
- audience recognizes the "losing focus" problem
- audience sees their role as reinforcing priorities
- it naturally leads into the practical example next
