# Slide 2 Specification

## Slide Purpose
This slide introduces the core analogy that explains the experience shown in Slide 1.

Slide 1 made the audience *feel* the problem.
Slide 2 gives them a simple, memorable way to understand it.

This slide should:
- label the experience
- make the problem intuitive
- create a mental model the audience will remember

It should NOT:
- explain the full system
- introduce technical concepts
- overload the audience with detail

## Core Idea (locked)
AI behaves like a helpful assistant that responds well in the moment, but does not reliably track the overall goal or narrative.

This is captured through the Dory analogy.

## On-Slide Text (locked)

Eagerly helpful  
Can’t follow the plot

## Visual Design

### Primary Visual
Use a large, centered image of:
entity["fictional_character","Dory","Finding Nemo character"]

The image should:
- be cleanly cropped
- have no busy background
- feel integrated into the slide aesthetic
- be large enough to dominate visual attention

### Layout
- Full-screen slide
- Minimal elements
- Dory centered or slightly above center
- Text either:
  - centered below the image, or
  - placed to one side with strong alignment

### Visual Principles
- lots of whitespace
- no clutter
- no additional icons or distractions
- no meme overlays on this slide

This is not a joke slide. It is a concept anchor.

## Typography
- very large font (>= 40pt equivalent)
- clean sans serif
- high contrast
- each line should feel like a headline

## Color
- neutral background (light or dark)
- high contrast text
- no red needed here (reserved for failure moments)

## Animation

### Entrance
- Dory image fades in first
- then text appears shortly after

### Behavior
- subtle fade or slight upward motion
- no dramatic animation
- calm, controlled pacing

This slide should feel like a pause after the chaos of Slide 1.

## Speaker Script (guideline)

The speaker should say approximately:

"This is what it feels like…  
it’s like building with Dory."

(pause)

"She’s always eager to help…"

(pause)

"But the second she sees a cool turtle,  
she completely forgets you’re trying to find Nemo."

(pause)

Optional bridge line (recommended):

"So every moment makes sense…  
but the story goes nowhere."

## Timing and Delivery
- allow a brief pause after introducing Dory
- allow the audience to recognize the character
- allow a beat for laughter/recognition after the turtle line

Do not rush this slide.

## Transition from Slide 1

Slide 1 ends in chaos (multiple Breaks).

Immediately after the final line from Slide 1:

"At this point… you’re not building anymore. You’re just trying to keep it from falling apart."

Then transition to Slide 2 and say:

"This is what it feels like… it’s like building with Dory."

The emotional shift should be:
- chaos → recognition → clarity

## Transition to Slide 3

Slide 3 will introduce the structural explanation (The Gap).

This slide should end on the idea that:
- things make sense moment-to-moment
- but the overall direction is lost

The optional line:
"the story goes nowhere"

should naturally lead into:
- works once vs buildable system

## Do Not
- do not add extra text
- do not explain technical causes
- do not add multiple images
- do not clutter the slide
- do not turn this into a meme collage
- do not over-animate

## Success Criteria
The slide is successful if:
- the audience instantly understands the analogy
- the audience laughs or nods in recognition
- the phrase "can’t follow the plot" sticks
- it creates a clear mental model for the rest of the talk
- it sets up the next slide without needing explanation
