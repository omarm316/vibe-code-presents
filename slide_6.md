# Slide 6 Specification

## Slide Purpose
This is the turning point of the talk.

Slides 1–5 showed:
- what the experience feels like
- why it feels that way
- what the consequence is
- that it happens across projects and tools
- that it breaks in repeatable patterns

Slide 6 answers:
**What has to change?**

This slide should not give tactics yet.
Its job is to reframe the audience’s role.

## Core Idea (locked)

The shift  
From prompting to directing

## On-Slide Text (locked)

From tasking  
To directing

Keep it to exactly two lines. No additional text.

## Visual Design (locked)

### Primary Visual: Left-to-Right Contrast
Show two contrasting visual states using the same basic building blocks.

#### Left side = Prompting
- scattered arrows
- disconnected blocks or cards
- elements feel locally responsive but not organized as a whole
- slight sense of fragmentation

#### Right side = Directing
- the same kinds of blocks or cards
- now arranged into a clean, connected structure
- one clear system with visible relationships
- visually calm and intentional

The visual message should be:
**same pieces, different level of control**

### Layout
- Full-screen slide
- Text centered near top or middle
- Left/right visual contrast sits beneath or around the text
- Strong whitespace
- Clear separation between left and right

### Visual Principles
- clean
- minimal
- no memes
- no icons unless they directly support the contrast
- same shapes on both sides so the difference is organization, not content

## Typography
- very large font (>= 40pt equivalent)
- bold or semi-bold
- clean sans serif
- each line treated as a headline

## Color
- neutral background
- high contrast text
- left side can use slightly more visual noise or lower contrast
- right side should feel cleaner and more stable
- do not overuse red here; this is a shift slide, not a failure slide

## Animation

### Entrance
- left side appears first
- right side appears second
- text appears last or between them, depending on pacing

### Behavior
Use a subtle transformation effect if possible:
- scattered elements on left
- then a smooth transition or crossfade into the structured right side

Do not make it flashy.
This should feel like a realization.

## Speaker Script (guideline)

Say approximately:

"Once you see these patterns…  
you realize the problem isn’t the feature."

(pause)

"It’s how you’re approaching the build."

(pause)

"Most people are just giving AI tasks."

"And that works… when you’re building something small enough to be one task."

(pause)

"But the moment you’re building a system that's more than a one-off…"

(pause)

"You have to start directing it."

Optional reinforcement line (recommended):

"AI builds features. You direct the system."

## Timing and Delivery
- this slide needs more pause than the previous one
- let the reframe land
- emphasize the word "directing"
- do not rush into the next principle immediately

## Transition from Slide 5

Slide 5 ends with:
"Once you see this… you realize the problem isn’t the feature."

Then continue:
"It’s how you’re directing the system."

Slide 6 appears here.

## Transition to Slide 7

After this reframe, move into the first principle:

"And that starts with giving the system a direction before you ask it to build anything."

This sets up the next slide about starting with direction.

## Do Not
- do not list tactics on this slide
- do not add bullet points
- do not over-explain
- do not use technical jargon
- do not clutter the left/right contrast

## Success Criteria
The slide is successful if:
- the audience feels a clear mental shift
- they understand the difference between prompting and directing
- the line "From prompting / To directing" sticks
- the next slides feel like a system, not random tips
