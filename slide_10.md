# Slide 10 Specification

## Slide Purpose
This is the final slide of the presentation.

Its job is to clearly state what the audience can now do that they could not do before the talk.

This slide should:
- mirror the opening promise
- convert the talk into concrete capabilities
- stay on screen during Q&A

This slide should NOT:
- summarize content
- introduce new ideas
- say "thank you"
- ask for questions

## Core Idea (locked)

What you can do now

## On-Slide Text (locked)

• Turn ideas into buildable MVPs  
• Keep AI systems from breaking as they grow  
• Direct AI instead of reacting to it  
• Build products—not just prototypes

Keep to exactly four bullets. No additional text.

## Visual Design (locked)

### Layout
- Full-screen slide
- Title centered at top
- Bullet list centered or slightly left-aligned in the middle
- Generous whitespace around all elements

### Visual Style
- very clean
- no diagrams
- no memes
- no icons unless extremely subtle (optional small checkmarks)

### Visual Principles
- simplicity over decoration
- high readability
- strong visual hierarchy

## Typography
- very large font (>= 40pt equivalent)
- bold or semi-bold title
- clean sans serif
- bullet text large and easily readable from distance

## Color
- neutral background
- high contrast text
- optional subtle accent for bullet markers
- avoid red (this is a confident closing, not a warning)

## Animation

### Entrance
- title fades in first
- bullets appear either all at once or one-by-one quickly

### Behavior
- no complex animations
- calm and confident

## Speaker Script (guideline)

Say approximately:

"So now… instead of just prompting AI…"

"You can actually build something that holds up."

(pause)

Then walk through bullets lightly:

"You can take an idea… and turn it into something you can actually build on."

"You can keep it from breaking as it grows."

"You can direct the system instead of reacting to it."

"And you can build products… not just prototypes."

(pause)

Final line:

"That’s the difference between something that works once… and something you can actually build into a real product."

## Timing and Delivery
- slow down slightly compared to earlier slides
- allow space between bullets
- do not rush the final line

## Transition from Slide 9

Slide 9 ends with the practical example and explanation of maintaining the system.

Then say:

"This is the difference between something that works once…"

"…and something you can actually keep building into a product."

Slide 10 appears.

## Post-Slide Behavior

This slide remains on screen during Q&A.

Do NOT replace it with:
- "Thank you"
- "Questions?"

## Do Not
- do not add extra bullets
- do not add summary text
- do not clutter the slide
- do not change wording of bullets

## Success Criteria
The slide is successful if:
- audience clearly understands what they can now do
- the bullets feel like new capabilities, not summaries
- the ending feels complete and confident
- the slide can remain on screen without explanation during Q&A
